About Rider

Your commute is usually about 20 minutes, but your train has stopped at the next station for no reason. Again. You get off and have to formulate a plan of attack by the time you walk up the stairs. How are you getting to work?


Ask Rider. It gives you the cost and estimated time of all your transportation options.


Enter your starting address and your destination address. Tapping Go brings up a list of local travel options from Uber, Lyft, Google Transit and Taxi Fare Finder. So you can order a car, take the train or bus, or call a taxi. The results list the price and the estimated time of arrival for each option.


Rider is designed to work through a browser on your smartphone—tapping the arrow on the results page opens the appropriate app or calls the appropriate company.


If you’re tech-inclined, you’ll be happy to know that Rider is an Angular app calling a separate API designed specifically for it, and you can learn more about FareFairy on its page. If you’re not, don’t worry: Rider makes travel easy, and that’s all you need to know.
